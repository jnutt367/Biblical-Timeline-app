In the Beginning
Creation of the Universe
Creation & Beginnings
"In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth." The foundation of all existence and God's perfect design.

God speaks the universe into existence through His divine Word, establishing the fundamental order of creation over six days and resting on the seventh, setting the pattern for work and rest.

"And God said, 'Let there be light,' and there was light. God saw that the light was good, and he separated the light from the darkness." - Genesis 1:3-4

Theological Significance: This establishes God as the sovereign Creator, the source of all life, and the foundation for understanding our purpose and relationship with Him. Everything that exists comes from His word and will.

For Today: We are fearfully and wonderfully made in God's image, giving us inherent dignity, purpose, and the calling to be stewards of His creation.

Genesis 3
The Fall of Humanity
Creation & Beginnings
Adam and Eve's disobedience introduces sin into the world, but God immediately promises redemption and hope.

The first humans, created in perfect fellowship with God, choose to disobey His command, bringing sin, death, and separation into creation. Yet God's love pursues them even in their rebellion.

"And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your offspring and hers; he will crush your head, and you will strike his heel." - Genesis 3:15

The First Gospel: Genesis 3:15 is called the Protoevangelium - God's first promise of a coming Savior who will defeat Satan and restore humanity to relationship with God.

Hope in Darkness: Even in judgment, God shows mercy by providing clothing and promising redemption through the woman's seed - pointing directly to Christ's future victory.

c. 2348 BC
Noah's Ark and the Great Flood
Old Testament
God judges the world's wickedness but preserves Noah's family, establishing His covenant of grace and faithfulness.

As humanity's wickedness grieves God's heart, He chooses to start anew while preserving righteous Noah and his family through the ark - a picture of salvation through faith and obedience.

"I establish my covenant with you: Never again will all life be destroyed by the waters of a flood; never again will there be a flood to destroy the earth." - Genesis 9:11

God's Justice and Mercy: The flood demonstrates God's holy judgment against sin, while the ark shows His mercy and plan for salvation. Noah found grace in the eyes of the Lord.

Covenant Promise: The rainbow covenant establishes God's faithfulness and introduces the pattern of divine covenants that culminate in Christ's new covenant.

c. 2000 BC
God's Call to Abraham
Old Testament
God calls Abram to leave his homeland and promises to make him a great nation through whom all peoples will be blessed.

God calls Abram from Ur of the Chaldeans, promising to make him the father of many nations and to bless all families of the earth through him - the beginning of God's chosen people.

"I will make you into a great nation, and I will bless you; I will make your name great, and you will be a blessing... and all peoples on earth will be blessed through you." - Genesis 12:2-3

The Abrahamic Covenant: This unconditional covenant establishes the foundation for God's redemptive plan, promising land, descendants, and universal blessing through Abraham's seed.

Faith and Righteousness: Abraham's faith is credited to him as righteousness (Romans 4:16), making him the father of all who believe and trust in God's promises.

c. 4 BC
The Birth of Jesus Christ
New Testament
The eternal Word becomes flesh as Jesus is born of the Virgin Mary, fulfilling centuries of prophetic promises and bringing hope to the world.

In the fullness of time, God sends His Son, born of a woman, to redeem humanity and fulfill all the promises made to Abraham, David, and the prophets throughout history.

"The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us. We have seen his glory, the glory of the one and only Son, who came from the Father, full of grace and truth." - John 1:14

The Incarnation: Jesus is fully God and fully man (the hypostatic union), uniquely qualified to be our mediator, substitute, and the bridge between heaven and earth.

Prophecy Fulfilled: His virgin birth, Bethlehem birthplace, and Davidic lineage fulfill specific Old Testament prophecies, proving God's faithfulness across generations.

c. 33 AD
The Crucifixion and Resurrection
New Testament
Jesus dies for our sins and rises from the dead, accomplishing salvation and defeating death forever - the central event of all history.

The central event of human history: Jesus bears the penalty for sin on the cross and conquers death through His resurrection, opening the way for eternal life with God.

"He was delivered over to death for our sins and was raised to life for our justification. Therefore, since we have been justified through faith, we have peace with God." - Romans 4:25-5:1

Substitutionary Atonement: Jesus takes our place, bearing God's wrath against sin so we can receive His righteousness and be reconciled to the Father.

Victory Over Death: The resurrection proves Jesus' deity, validates His sacrifice, and guarantees our future resurrection and eternal life with Him.

New Creation: Christ's resurrection begins the new creation, making all things new for those who believe and trust in Him as Lord and Savior.

Future
The Second Coming of Christ
Prophetic Future
Jesus will return in glory to judge the living and the dead, establishing His eternal kingdom and making all things new.

Christ will return visibly and gloriously to complete His redemptive work, judge all humanity, and establish the new heavens and new earth where righteousness dwells.

"Look, he is coming with the clouds, and every eye will see him, even those who pierced him; and all peoples on earth will mourn because of him. So shall it be! Amen." - Revelation 1:7

The Blessed Hope: The Second Coming is the Christian's blessed hope, when we will be glorified, sin will be finally defeated, and we will reign with Christ forever.

Final Judgment: Christ will judge all people according to their response to the Gospel, separating the righteous from the wicked for eternity.

Kingdom Consummated: God's kingdom will be fully established, and He will dwell with His people forever in perfect peace, joy, and righteousness.
